Introduction

Outlier Detection and Removal

This topic usually requires 3–4 lectures because it is extremely important in Machine Learning.

The goal of this lecture is to build a strong conceptual understanding before learning the practical coding implementation.

What are Outliers?

Definition

An outlier is a data point or observation that is significantly different from the rest of the observations in the dataset.

It behaves unusually compared to the majority of the data.

Simple Examples

1

The Classroom

Imagine a classroom. Almost every student scores around 40–60 marks.

42 48 55 51 59 45 100

One student scores 100. That student becomes an outlier because he is very different from everyone else.

"Sharma Ji's son" — who always scores much higher than the entire class. 😄

2

The Salary Dataset

Suppose everyone's monthly salary is:

PersonSalary (₹)
Person A15,000
Person B18,000
Person C20,000
Person D22,000
Person E19,000
Bill Gates₹Billions

Although Bill Gates is a real person, his salary does not represent the typical distribution of the dataset. Therefore, his salary becomes an outlier.

Why are Outliers Dangerous?

Outliers can distort the dataset.

Machine Learning algorithms try to learn patterns from data. If a few observations are extremely different, many algorithms become biased toward those observations.

Poor model performance

Wrong relationships learned

Poor predictions on new data

Regression Example

Suppose we have a dataset. Input: Hours Studied. Target: Exam Marks.

Normally: More Study Hours → Higher Marks

Normal Data — Good Fit

Hours Studied →

Marks →

A linear regression line would fit nicely.

Now imagine two exceptional students:

Hours = 1

Marks = 98

Hours = 2

Marks = 95

They are outliers. The regression model now tries to fit these unusual points.

With Outliers — Wrong Fit

Wrong regression line

Poor predictions

Poor model performance

Why Does This Happen?

Linear Regression minimizes error using least squares. Large errors receive larger penalties.

Therefore, outliers pull the regression line toward themselves.

Linear Regression is highly sensitive to outliers.

Hidden Danger

Outliers are dangerous because they are often hidden. They don't always look obvious. Yet they silently reduce model performance.

Are Outliers Always Bad?

No.

This is one of the biggest misconceptions. Sometimes outliers should be removed. Sometimes they are the most valuable part of the dataset.

A Data Scientist must understand the difference.

When Should You Remove Outliers?

Remove outliers if they are caused by:

1

Data Entry Errors

Example — Age column:

23 31 45 29 830

Age = 830 is impossible. It is clearly a typing mistake. Since we don't know the correct age, the safest choice is usually to remove that observation.

2

Measurement Errors

Broken sensor Faulty thermometer Wrong GPS reading Corrupted database Incorrect manual entry

These should usually be removed.

When Should You NOT Remove Outliers?

Sometimes outliers contain important information.

1

Credit Card Fraud Detection

Suppose most transactions are:

₹500 ₹800 ₹1,000

One transaction is:

₹5,00,000

That transaction is an outlier. But that is exactly what the fraud detection model wants to identify.

Removing it would remove the fraud itself. Do NOT remove it.

2

The Exceptional Students

In the previous student example, the students who scored very high with little study might actually have:

Extremely high IQ
Previous knowledge
Better memory
Exceptional intelligence

Instead of removing them, we can add another feature:

IQ

Now the model may understand why those students performed differently. The outliers become explainable.

Key Lesson

Detecting outliers is easy. Deciding what to do with them is much harder.

You must decide whether to:

Keep them
Remove them
Modify them
Explain them via features

This depends entirely on the business problem.

Effect on ML Algorithms

Some algorithms are highly affected. Others are naturally robust.

Highly Sensitive Algorithms

Weight-Based

Linear Regression

Regression line gets pulled toward outliers.

Weight-Based

Logistic Regression

Decision boundary changes. Predictions become worse.

Probabilistic

Naive Bayes

Can be affected because parameter estimates become distorted.

Weight-Based

Deep Learning / Neural Networks

Large outliers can create huge gradients. Training becomes unstable. Loss increases. Optimization becomes difficult.

General rule: If an algorithm learns by calculating weights, it is usually more sensitive to outliers.

Less Sensitive Algorithms

Tree-based algorithms are much more robust. Why? Because trees split data based on conditions rather than fitting a continuous mathematical equation.

Decision Tree

Random Forest

Gradient Boosting

XGBoost

LightGBM

CatBoost

Extreme values usually have much less influence on tree splits.

Ways to Treat Outliers

There are four common approaches.

1

Trimming (Removal)

The simplest method. Simply delete the outlier.

Very easy

Very fast

Data loss

Risk of removing useful information

2

Capping (Winsorization)

Instead of removing, replace extreme values with boundary values.

Before (Max = 90)

After

20 40 60 85 90 90

Similarly, very small values can also be capped. Example — Minimum allowed = 10:

Before

-15 5 12

After

10 10 12

This is one of the most commonly used techniques in industry.

3

Treat Outliers as Missing Values

Replace outliers with NaN, then apply missing value imputation techniques:

Mean Imputation Median Imputation KNN Imputer MICE Iterative Imputation
4

Discretization (Binning)

Convert numerical values into intervals.

Age (Continuous)

18 21 25 28 31 35 90

Age (Binned)

0–20 20–30 30–40 40–100

Now 90 is grouped into the highest interval. Its influence is reduced.

Industry mostly uses: Trimming and Capping (Winsorization). The remaining methods are used less frequently.

How to Detect Outliers

There are many methods. This course focuses on the three most important ones.

Method 1

Standard Deviation Method (3-Sigma Rule)

Use this only when data is approximately Normally Distributed (Gaussian).

Remember the Empirical Rule:

±1σ

68.27% of data

±2σ

95.45% of data

±3σ

99.73% of data

If any observation lies outside Mean ± 3 × Standard Deviation, it can be treated as an outlier.

Formula — 3-Sigma Rule

Lower Limit: μ − 3σ

Upper Limit: μ + 3σ

Where μ = Mean, σ = Standard Deviation

Method 2

IQR Method (Box Plot Rule)

Use this when data is skewed (not normally distributed). It works with Box Plots.

Definitions

Q1 = 25th percentile

Q3 = 75th percentile

IQR = Q3 − Q1

Box Plot Visualization

Outlier

Lower Fence

Q1

Median

Q3

Upper Fence

Outlier

Outlier Boundaries

Lower Limit: Q1 − 1.5 × IQR

Upper Limit: Q3 + 1.5 × IQR

Any value outside these limits is considered an outlier. This is one of the most widely used methods in practice.

Method 3

Percentile Method

This method works for almost any distribution. Choose cutoff percentiles:

1st – 99th

5th – 95th

2.5th – 97.5th

Values outside these boundaries are treated as outliers. The threshold depends on the business problem.

Interview Questions

1. What is an outlier in machine learning?

An outlier is a data point that is significantly different from the rest of the observations in the dataset. It behaves unusually compared to the majority of the data and can be caused by errors or can represent genuine rare events.

2. Why are outliers harmful?

Outliers can distort the dataset. ML algorithms try to learn patterns from data, and extreme values can bias the model toward those observations — leading to poor performance, wrong relationships, and bad predictions on new data.

3. Are outliers always bad? Explain with examples.

No. In fraud detection, the outlier IS the fraud — removing it destroys the purpose. In anomaly detection, outliers are the target. However, outliers caused by data entry errors (e.g., Age = 830) or measurement errors should be removed as they add noise, not information.

4. Difference between trimming and capping.

Trimming completely removes outlier observations from the dataset — causes data loss. Capping (Winsorization) replaces extreme values with boundary limits (e.g., any value above 90th percentile becomes 90) — no data loss, retains the row but modifies the extreme value.

5. Which ML algorithms are sensitive to outliers?

Any weight-based algorithm is sensitive: Linear Regression (line gets pulled), Logistic Regression (boundary shifts), Neural Networks (huge gradients, unstable training), Naive Bayes (distorted parameters). The common factor: they all compute weights/parameters that are influenced by every data point.

6. Why are tree-based models less affected by outliers?

Trees split data based on conditions (thresholds) rather than fitting a continuous mathematical equation. An outlier at value 500 vs 12 in a split like "X > 10" — both fall on the same side. The exact magnitude doesn't change the split point much, unlike a regression coefficient.

7. Explain the 3-Sigma Rule.

In a normal distribution, 99.73% of data falls within Mean ± 3σ. Any observation outside this range (below μ−3σ or above μ+3σ) is considered an outlier. This only works well for approximately normally distributed data.

8. Explain the IQR method for detecting outliers.

Calculate IQR = Q3 − Q1. Any value below Q1 − 1.5 × IQR or above Q3 + 1.5 × IQR is an outlier. This works well for skewed distributions and is visualized through box plots. It's one of the most commonly used methods.

9. What is Winsorization?

Winsorization is a capping technique that replaces extreme values with percentile-based boundary values. For example, at the 5th and 95th percentile: values below 5th percentile are replaced with the 5th percentile value, and values above 95th are replaced with the 95th percentile value. No data is removed.

10. When should outliers be removed, and when should they be retained?

Remove when caused by data entry errors, measurement errors, or impossible values (e.g., negative age). Retain when they represent genuine rare events — fraud detection, anomaly detection, rare diseases, or when they can be explained by adding more features. Always understand the business context first.

Data Scientist's Notes

Extra

Outliers can be legitimate (real observations) or erroneous (caused by mistakes). Always investigate before removing them.

Visualization is often the first step in detection. Common plots include box plots, scatter plots, histograms, KDE plots, and violin plots.

Some advanced algorithms for multivariate outlier detection include Isolation Forest, Local Outlier Factor (LOF), One-Class SVM, DBSCAN, and Elliptic Envelope. These are useful when outliers depend on relationships between multiple features rather than a single column.

In real-world projects, outlier handling should be done after understanding the business context and only on the training data to avoid data leakage.

There is no universal rule for handling outliers. The correct approach depends on the data distribution, domain knowledge, and the objective of the machine learning problem.
